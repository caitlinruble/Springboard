    Help

    How does the Global Atlas work?

    The Global Atlas for Renewable Energy contains over 2000 datasets from 50 partners that are available for access as layers to the user. These layers contain information on renewable resources or supporting data like roads, power lines, and substations. The user can overlay different types of information and select or unselect each dataset to customise the view. Some of the layers focus on a specific country or region, and some show global data. Users can also add the URL to an external layer to access that external layer, as well as to create and save their own maps.

    The Global Atlas also includes integrated tools that allow the user to simulate real life situations for renewable project's assessment, such as PVWatts solar PV system simulator and bioenergy simulator.

    Where does the data come from?

    The data in the Global Atlas is provided by partner organisations including governments, research institutes and private companies. The Global Atlas for Renewable Energy provides access to resource data with different coverage areas and resolutions, created using different methodologies. In general, the data in the Atlas does not result from direct measurements but is estimated using advanced data processing methods. These estimates are then validated using an accepted methodology, such as comparison with actual ground measurements.

    How to use the Global Atlas?

    The Global Atlas provides users with two different data browsing structures:

    - Point query, which enables users to get resource or supporting data, such as the distance to the grid and substation, distance to the road, land features, slope, elevation, or population density, for a selected location on the map

    - Layer query, which enables users to either create a new map or edit an existing map using the available layers within IRENA's repository

    How to query data at a specific point?

    The Global Atlas allows the users to query a renewable energy resource data and supporting information at specific location using the Point query functionality. To get the data:

    - Select a renewable resource

    - Enter a location or click on the map

    - Click on the "Get data" button to display the results

    - Click on the "Download" button to export the results

    How to query (or search) for a layer?

    The Global Atlas allows users to search for a renewable energy resource layer from the Layer query functionality. To add layer to the GIS platform:

    - Type your preferences (keywords, URL, or country) in the "Search" tab

    - Click on the "Get data" button to display the results

    - Filter the returned layers based on use (education, policy, potential, and business), if desired

    - Select the layer of interest from the returned search results

    - Click on "Add to map" to add the layer of interest to the GIS platform

    How to quick access data?

    The Global Atlas provides various tools allowing users to quick retrieve point-specific information and basic statistics of the active layers through the info picker functionality. To activate the info picker:

    - Click on "Info picker" from the three dots menu on the right side of the screen

    - Enter a location (coordinates) or click on the map in the floating dialog box

    - Click on the "Get data" button to display the results

    How to measure a distance or area?

    The Global Atlas allows users to conduct some measurements on a map. To measure the distance (area):

    - Click on "Measure" from the three dots menu on the right side of the screen

    - Click on several points on the maps to define a line (area) connecting these points

    - A user interface dialog will pop up to display the total distance (area) between the points

    How to manipulate a map?

    The Global Atlas provides users with different functionalities to manipulate the layers in the GIS platform:

    - Edit, which allow users to view, hide, rename, remove, zoom, and download the layers

    - Clip, which allows users to clip data over a selected area or country

    - Draw, which enables users to mark out points, lines, or areas on a map

    - Save, which enables users to save a map under their user's account

    How to create (save) your own maps?

    The Global Atlas allows users to create and save maps within their registered account such that they can be accessed with ease under My Maps tab. To save your own map:

    - Type your preferences (keywords, URL, or country) in the "Search" tab and click on "Get data"

    - Click on "Add to map" to add the layer of interest to the GIS platform from the returned results

    - Manipulate the layers using the functionalities provided (Edit, Clip, and Draw)

    - Sign up/Sign in to your account to be able to Save the map

    How to customise a pre-existing map?

    The Global Atlas allows users to customise their existing map through the Layer query. To edit an existing map:

    - Click on "Sign in" from the top right corner of the page and sign in to your account

    - Click on "Add to map" to display the selected map

    - Select layer to Edit (rename, remove, zoom, opacity), Clip, or Draw

    - Click on "Save"

    How to edit (save) a legend?

    The Global Atlas allows users to render a visual legend object for each layer in the same sequence of the map layer hierarchy. The legend identifies key features in the map and adopts changes in the layer sequence or layer configuration. If possible, a layer legend tries to show value units. To edit a legend:

    - Click on "Legend" from the top right corner of the GIS platform

    - Click on "Edit legend" to adjust the legend style - classifications' colours and opacity

    - Click on "Apply changes" to save the legend through the GIS platform

    How to download a layer?

    The Global Atlas permits users to obtain the raster or vector data of selected layers in IRENAs published data. To download the layers:

    - Type your preferences (keywords, URL, or country) in the "Search" tab and click on "Get data"

    - Filter the returned layers based on use (education, policy, potential, and business), if desired

    - Select the layer of interest from the returned search results Click on "Download" to export the data

    How to share maps on social media?

    The Global Atlas provides a sharing option where users can share a map on social media with the preferred options being LinkedIn, Facebook, and Twitter. This option also includes sharing the HTML code for integrating a minimised version of the Global Atlas with the currently loaded map into an external website. To share a map as displayed on the screen through social media:

    - Click on the "Share" button at the top right corner of the screen

    - Select the social media icon or "Embed" option

    How to create an account?

    The Global Atlas is accessible to all users online but provides a more personalised experience with additional features for registered users. To register on the platform:

    - Click on "Sign in" from the top right corner of the page

    - Click on "Sign up now" at the bottom of the login window to create an account

    How to reset your password?

    The Global Atlas allows users to reset their account's password or change it if the user forgets the password. To reset your password:

    - Click on "Sign in" at the top right corner of the page

    - Click on "Forgot your password?" at the bottom right corner of the dialog box

    - Insert your email to receive further instructions

    How to update your account?

    The Global Atlas provides users with the option to update their account or edit its settings. To update your account:

    - "Sign in" to your account

    - Select "Edit account" from the dropdown menu

    - Edit and save your account settings

    About

    The Global Atlas for Renewable energy is an online platform intended to help users - policymakers and investors - to find renewable energy resources maps for locations across the world.

    The Global Atlas stems from an initiative coordinated by IRENA and aimed at closing the gap between countries that have access to the necessary data and expertise to evaluate the potential for renewable energy deployment in their countries and those that lack these elements.

    The initiative has brought together more than 50 highly skilled international research institutions to share over 2000 renewable energy maps on this single and consistent platform covering solar, wind, bioenergy, geothermal and marine energy.

    Reach out to the Global Atlas at IRENA to contribute data, expertise, or to promote the Global Atlas initiative.
